Pickering (Martian crater)

Pickering Crater is a crater in the Phaethontis quadrangle on Mars , located at 33.1° south latitude and 132.5° west longitude. It is 115 kilometres (71 mi) in diameter.

Pickering Crater is a crater in the Phaethontis quadrangle on Mars , located at 33.1° south latitude and 132.5° west longitude. It is 115 kilometres (71 mi) in diameter and was named after several people: Edward Charles, American astronomer (1846–1919); William Henry, American astronomer (1858–1938); and Sir William Hayward, New Zealand-American engineer (1910–2004). It is 115 kilometres (71 mi) in diameter and was named after several people: Edward Charles, American astronomer (1846-1919); William Henry, American astronomer (1858-1938); and Sir William Hayward, New Zealand-American engineer (1910-2004).
