Mars Pathfinder

Mars Pathfinder ( MESUR Pathfinder ) [1] [4] is an American robotic spacecraft that landed a base station with a roving probe on Mars in 1997. It consisted of a lander , renamed the Carl Sagan Memorial Station , and a lightweight (10.6 kg/23 lb) wheeled robotic Mars rover named Sojourner , [5] which became the first rover to operate outside the Earth–Moon system.

The Mars Pathfinder , launched in December 4 , 1996 by NASA , was a hugely successful Mars exploration probe . Though completely successful and completing real objectives, the Mars Pathfinder mission can be regarded as a "proof-of-concept" for various technologies, such as aerobraking , airbag -mediated touchdown, and automated obstacle avoidance, all later exploited by the Mars Exploration Rovers . The Mars Pathfinder was also remarkable for its extremely low price, relative to other unmanned space missions. This was an important consideration, considering approximately two-thirds of spacecraft destined to Mars either fail to launch or are lost enroute (See Mars Climate Orbiter in particular.)
