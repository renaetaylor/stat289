Minor planet

A minor planet is an astronomical object in direct orbit around the Sun (or more broadly, any star with a planetary system ) that is neither a planet nor exclusively classified as a comet . [a] Before 2006 the International Astronomical Union (IAU) officially used the term minor planet , but during that year's meeting it reclassified minor planets and comets into dwarf planets and small Solar System bodies (SSSBs). [1]

Minor planet is a term used since the 19th century to describe objects, such as asteroids , that are in orbit around the Sun but are not planets or comets . [1] The term planetoid has also been used, especially for larger objects. [2] Historically, the terms asteroid , minor planet , and planetoid have been more or less synonymous, [3] [2] but the issue has been complicated by the discovery of numerous minor planets beyond the orbit of Jupiter and especially Neptune that are not universally considered asteroids.
