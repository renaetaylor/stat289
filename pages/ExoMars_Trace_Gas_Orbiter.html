ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter

The ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter ( TGO or ExoMars Orbiter ) is a collaborative project between the European Space Agency (ESA) and Roscosmos that sent an atmospheric research orbiter and the Schiaparelli demonstration lander to Mars in 2016 as part of the European-led ExoMars programme. [6] [7] [8]

2013 Mars Science Orbiter is the name for a standing and flexible proposal within NASA to send a new orbiter to Mars . [1] If launched, it would be the successor to the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter , which may still be operational at that time. It would be launched in 2013 . A range of atmospheric and surface observations have been proposed, including small landers. [2]
