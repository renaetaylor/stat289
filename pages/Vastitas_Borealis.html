Vastitas Borealis

Vastitas Borealis ( Latin , 'northern waste' [1] ) is the largest lowland region of Mars . It is in the northerly latitudes of the planet and encircles the northern polar region. [2] Vastitas Borealis is often simply referred to as the northern plains , northern lowlands or the North polar erg [3] of Mars. The plains lie 4–5 km below the mean radius of the planet, and is centered at 87°44′N 32°32′E ﻿ / ﻿ 87.73°N 32.53°E ﻿ / 87.73; 32.53 . [4] To the north lies Planum Boreum . A small part of Vastitas Borealis lies in the Ismenius Lacus quadrangle .
