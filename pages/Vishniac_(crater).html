Vishniac (crater)

Vishniac is the larger crater of the Martian surface feature called the Giant's Footprint . It was named after Wolf V. Vishniac , a microbiologist who died on an expedition to Antarctica . Fittingly, the crater lies in the Antarctic of Mars. [1] The feature was originally observed by Mariner 7 in 1969. In 1999, the Mars Global Surveyor 's Mars Orbiter Camera was able to provide more detailed pictures. [2] The crater measures approximately 80 kilometers in diameter. Its name was approved by the International Astronomical Union in 1976. [3]
