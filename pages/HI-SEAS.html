The Hawaii Space Exploration Analog and Simulation ( HI-SEAS ) is an analog habitat for human spaceflight to Mars . [1] [2] [3] HI-SEAS is located in an isolated position on the slopes of the Mauna Loa volcano on the island of Hawaii . The area has Mars-like features and an elevation of approximately 8,200 feet (2,500 m) above sea level. The first HI-SEAS study was in 2013 and NASA 's Human Research Program continues to fund and sponsor follow-up studies. [4] The missions are of extended duration from four months to a year.

HI-SEAS (Hawaii Space Exploration Analog and Simulation), is a human spaceflight analog focused on culinary and pyschologial aspects. [1] Many related aspects are also explored, including temperatures in artificial habitats. [2] It was orchestrated primarily by NASA , University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa , and Cornell University . [3] The first study was in 2013, and NASA has sponsored follow-on studies. [4] The hab is located at 8,000 feet altitude on the island of Mauna Loa . [4] The 2013 study includes 8 people and runs for 120 days (4 months). [3] Its a Mars analog habitat , and they have to wear simulated spacesuits when they go outside. [3] The focus is on diet, which consits of traditional space food (such as freeze-dried items) as well various recipes made from a special list of ingredients. [3]
