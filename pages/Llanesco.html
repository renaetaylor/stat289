Llanesco

Llanesco is a Martian impact crater, 27 kilometers in diameter. It is located at 28.5S, 101.2°W, north of the crater Dinorwic . It is named after a town in Spain , and its name was approved by the International Astronomical Union in 1991. [1] [2] According to a surface age map of Mars based on US Geological Survey data, the area around Llanesco is from the Noachian or Hesperian epoch , which places the area's age at 3.8 to 1.8 billion years. [3] The crater's rim averages about 7,750 meters above zero altitude, and its floor averages about 7,000 meters above zero altitude. The crater is therefore approximately only 750 meters deep. [2]
