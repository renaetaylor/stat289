(311999) 2007 NS 2 was discovered on July 14, 2007, by the Observatorio Astronómico de La Sagra . [4] [5] [6] [7] [8] Its orbit is characterized by low eccentricity (0.054), moderate inclination (18.6°) and a semi-major axis of 1.52 AU. [8] Upon discovery, it was classified as Mars-crosser by the Minor Planet Center . Its orbit is well determined as it is currently (March 2013) based on 87 observations with a data-arc span of 4,800 days. [2] 2007 NS 2 has an absolute magnitude of 17.8, which gives a characteristic diameter of 870 m. [2]
