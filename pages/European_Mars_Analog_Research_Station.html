The European Mars Analog Research Station (EuroMARS) is a project operated by the Mars Society to conduct geological and biological exploration under constraints similar to those found on Mars, to develop field tactics based on those explorations, to test habitat design features and tools, and to assess crew selection protocols. Unlike the Flashline Mars Arctic Research Station and the Mars Desert Research Station , which were chosen for their physical similarity to Mars , EuroMARS will be set up on Krafka , a volcanic rift in north-east Iceland . Scheduled to begin operation in 2006 , this third project will benefit from the Society's experiences with the first two stations. For instance, the new habitat will have three levels instead of two, to reduce traffic conflicts between living quarters, laboratory, and airlock.
