Perepelkin (Martian crater)

Perepelkin Crater is an impact crater in the Arcadia quadrangle of the planet Mars . It is located at 52.8°N latitude and 64.6°W longitude. It is 112.0  km in diameter. It was named after Yevgeny Perepyolkin ; the name was approved in 1973 by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) Working Group for Planetary System Nomenclature (WGPSN). [1] Nearby named craters include the Maricourt to the west and the larger Barabashov to the south-southwest with the estimate distance of 650 km, also located there are hills known as Mareotis Fossae .

Perepelkin Crater is an impact crater in the Arcadia quadrangle of the planet Mars . It is located at 52.8°N latitude and 64.6°W longitude. It is 112.0  km in diameter. It was named after Yevgeny Perepyolkin ; the name was approved in 1973 by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) Working Group for Planetary System Nomenclature (WGPSN). [1] Much of the crater is covered with a mantle that is believed to be ice-rich and to have fallen from the atmosphere when the climate was different. In one of the images below mantle can be seen; also some places when the mantle has disappeared, channels are visible.
