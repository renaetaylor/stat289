South (Martian crater)

South is an impact crater in the Mare Australe quadrangle of Mars , located at 77.1°S latitude and 338.0°W longitude. It measures 101.84 kilometers in diameter and was named after British astronomer Sir James South (1785–1867). The name was approved in 1973, by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) Working Group for Planetary System Nomenclature . [1] The crater's western rim touches the Australe Montes (Southern Martian Mountains), north of the crater is Sisyphi Planum . To the east is the crater Main .
