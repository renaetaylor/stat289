Noachis quadrangle

The Noachis quadrangle is one of a series of 30 quadrangle maps of Mars used by the United States Geological Survey (USGS) Astrogeology Research Program . The Noachis quadrangle is also referred to as MC-27 (Mars Chart-27). [1]

The Noachis quadrangle covers the area from 300° to 360° west longitude and 30° to 65° south latitude on Mars . It lies between the two giant impact basins on Mars: Argyre and Hellas. Noachis is so densely covered with craters that it is considered among the oldest places on Mars. When a location on Mars is as old, it is said to be Noachian in age.
