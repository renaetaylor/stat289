Ascraeus Mons

Ascraeus Mons is a large shield volcano located in the Tharsis region of the planet Mars . It is the northernmost and tallest of three shield volcanoes collectively known as the Tharsis Montes .

Ascraeus Mons is the northernmost of three volcanos (collectively known as Tharsis Montes ) on the Tharsis bulge near the equator of the planet Mars . To its south is Pavonis Mons , and south of that is Arsia Mons . The highest volcano in the solar system , Olympus Mons , is to the northwest. The three Tharsis Montes, together with some smaller volcanoes to the north form a rather straight line. This arrangement suggests that they were formed by a crustal plate moving over a hot spot. Such an arrangement exists in the Earth's Pacific Ocean, especially with the Hawaiian Islands. The Hawaiian Islands are in a straight line, with the youngest in the south and the oldest in the north. So geologists believe the plate is moving while a plume of hot magma rises, then punches through the crust to produce a volcanic mountain.
