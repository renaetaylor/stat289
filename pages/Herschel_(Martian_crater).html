Herschel (Martian crater)

Herschel is a 304 kilometer Impact Basin [1] in the Martian southern hemisphere, at 14.5°S, 130°E, located in the Mare Tyrrhenum region [2] of Mars and is inside Terra Cimmeria . The crater is jointly named after the seventeenth/eighteenth century father and son astronomers William Herschel and John Herschel . [3]
