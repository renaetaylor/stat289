Keeler (Martian crater)

Keeler is an impact crater on Mars , located at 61.0°S latitude and 151.3°W longitude in the Phaethontis quadrangle . It is 95.0  km in diameter and was named after James Edward Keeler , and the name was approved in 1973. [1] The first image below shows the relationship among three craters that are near each other. Keeler Crater is to the North of Trumpler Crater . After Keeler was formed, a later impact formed Trumpler Crater, and in the process destroyed part of Keeler.
